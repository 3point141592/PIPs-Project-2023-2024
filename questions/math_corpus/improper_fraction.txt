= Improper fraction =
An improper fraction , also called a top-heavy fraction in Britain, is a fraction whose absolute value is greater than 1.  For example,  $ \displaystyle \frac 7 3 $  is an improper fraction.  Fractions which are not improper are called proper fractions . Improper fractions may be used as a counting system, and can also be used in cryptography. See improper fractional base .
