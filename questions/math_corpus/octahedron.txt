== Definition ==
In Euclidean geometry , an octahedron is any polyhedron with eight faces .  The term is most frequently to refer to a polyhedron with eight triangular faces, with four meeting at each vertex . 
The regular  octahedron has eight equilateral triangle faces and is one of the five Platonic solids .  It has six vertices, twelve edges, and is  dual to the cube . The regular octahedron can be decomposed into two square  pyramids by a plane constructed perpendicular to the space diagonal joining two opposite vertices.
